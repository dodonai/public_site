This form is decided on function, not diagnosis — the question is whether your condition keeps you from doing things that add up to work. The reviewer is trained to be skeptical, so the most common way a strong claim gets weakened is sounding too capable. Describe a realistic day — including the bad parts, the rest breaks, and what you've stopped doing. Be specific and concrete ("I can walk about a block before my leg gives out and I rest 10 minutes"), not vague ("I can't walk far"). Don't exaggerate, but don't minimize either — every honest limitation is a reason you'd be an unreliable employee, and that is what wins these cases.
