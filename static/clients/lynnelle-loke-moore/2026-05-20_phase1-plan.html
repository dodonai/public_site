— What we're building

Three pieces that replace the manual Phase 1 workflow

Today, the path from Decision Vault intake to a Phase 1 deliverable is hand-keyed. We're replacing the keying with three connected components, all driven by the v1.4 schema you already wrote.

01 · Intake

Custom intake form

Replaces Decision Vault for the Medicaid pathway only. 11 sections, branching on married/single and care-setting goal. Lives on lokemoorelaw.com.

02 · Worksheet

Worksheet generator

Auto-populates a copy of your multi-sheet .xlsx template — Copay couple, Copay individual, Spend Down, SPRA, Burn Rate. Same formulas, hand-editable after.

03 · Letter

Strategy letter drafter

Produces a .docx first draft with conditional sections turned on/off by calculated flags. Bones are right; your voice-editing lives in "Our Recommendations."

OneDrive is the system of record. Microsoft 365 stays your data home. Model-swappable, not Anthropic-locked.

— Decisions for today

Four schema gaps to resolve at kickoff

These need a call before code is written. Each one is small in isolation; together they shape the form and the worksheet layout.

  1. Multiple accounts per asset class. Your anonymized example has 5 Security Service FCU accounts and a USAA savings as separate lines. Schema collapses to one checking_savings_balance. Itemized = more defensible to HHSC. Roll-up = faster. Itemized sub-tables or roll-up totals?
  2. 4-date balance series. Spend Down tab tracks balances on Prior-mo / Snapshot / +1 mo / +2 mo. Schema only captures one snapshot. HHSC requirement or internal-only?
  3. Multiple pensions per spouse. Anonymized case shows OPM Pension + DFAS military pension as separate lines. Schema has one applicant_pension_gross. Extend to a repeating pension table?
  4. NH cost basis on Burn Rate tab. Template bakes in $5,232 / $6,935 / $7,750 for SA semi-private / private / your-office-recent-private. Form field, or always default to one?

One architecture question

  1. Worksheet or engine as source of truth? The letter quotes values that also appear on the worksheet. Two options: (a) engine computes once in code, writes to both — consistent by construction, but a hand-edited worksheet formula won't flow to the letter; (b) engine writes worksheet, letter drafter reads values back from the xlsx — worksheet stays canonical even after manual edits, slightly more brittle implementation. Recommend (a) for build simplicity, with the agreement that post-generation worksheet edits trigger a letter re-draft rather than hand-syncing.

Two workflow questions

  1. Where do financial numbers come from today? Decision Vault carries demographics but no financials. Are you keying them in by hand from client statements? Drives whether the new form is filled by the client, by Angie, or by both.
  2. Skill-share path for /phase1-deliverables. Recommend a GitHub repo we set up — becomes the working repo for the engagement. 60-second walkthrough at kickoff.
