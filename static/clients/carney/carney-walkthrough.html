1.What we heard on the call

The core question — "should we summarize each section or quote it verbatim?" — got a more nuanced answer than a global toggle. Here's how we're reading the rule you described:

The Rule

Verbatim for plain-English clinical narrative. Summarize everything else.

Specifically: within admission summaries, discharge summaries, and consultations, the History of Present Illness and Assessment sections should be quoted verbatim. Everything else — chief complaint headers, ROS checkboxes, vitals, medications lists, MRI findings, daily progress notes, nursing notes — should be summarized.

Why this split: the HPI and Assessment are written in real English by a resident, PA, or attending — preserving the original wording is "gold." But sections like "Today's Visit Continued · cefazolin 2g IV · midazolam IV push · fentanyl patch" read as a grocery list when extracted verbatim. Those are better summarized.
Our litmus test for any section: was this written as readable English by a human author? If yes, verbatim. If no (checkbox forms, vitals tables, med lists, structured templates), summarize.

John Doe was admitted under the care of hospitalist Dr. Jennifer Lee for progressive bilateral lower-extremity weakness, paresthesias, and gait instability following recent influenza vaccination. (Ex. 14, p. 201.)

History of Present Illness verbatim

"63 y/o M presents with 1-week history of progressive bilateral foot numbness ascending into both legs, with worsening gait instability and lower extremity weakness. Examination, Diagnostics, & Treatment summarized

Initial neurologic examination demonstrated bilateral lower-extremity weakness (4/5) with diminished patellar and Achilles reflexes. (Ex. 14, p. 209.) Lumbar puncture demonstrated elevated CSF protein with normal cell count consistent with albuminocytologic dissociation. (Ex. 14, p. 212.) Five-day course of IVIG was initiated. (Ex. 14, p. 215.)

Assessment verbatim

"Acute inflammatory demyelinating polyneuropathy, consistent with Guillain-Barré Syndrome. Onset within approximately one week of influenza vaccination, with no alternative inciting trigger identified. Findings of ascending weakness, hyporeflexia, abnormal EMG/NCS demonstrating demyelinating pattern, and elevated CSF protein support the diagnosis." (Ex. 14, p. 219.)

Plan & Disposition summarized

Treatment plan included completion of IVIG course, daily neurologic monitoring, physical and occupational therapy evaluations, fall precautions, and transition to inpatient rehabilitation. (Ex. 14, p. 221.)

The "gold" causation language from the assessment is preserved verbatim — quotable as-is for petition drafting. The repetitive boilerplate around exam findings, treatments, and admin details is summarized to keep the entry readable.

5.Handwritten records — our proposed approach

You flagged the Suntree Chiropractic handwritten notes coming back as "no relevant content" — and noted that these can contain critical information (like a patient's first report of symptoms relative to vaccination). Here's what we'd like to propose, rather than asking you to design the policy.

Our proposed handling for handwritten / low-quality OCR records

  1. Always attempt extraction — never silently drop a handwritten record. Run best-effort OCR and pull what we can read.
  2. Flag the entry with a visible badge like "Handwritten record — manual review recommended" so you know at a glance that the entry should be eyeballed against the source.
  3. When OCR is unreadable, still surface the entry with the date, provider, and a placeholder note like "Handwritten chiropractic note dated 12/27/2024 — content not machine-readable; see Exhibit X, p. Y." Better to know it exists than for it to disappear from the chronology.
  4. Best-effort summary even of partial content — e.g., if we can read "symptom onset 11/29" but not the surrounding paragraph, surface that fragment with a note that the rest is handwritten.

If this approach works for you, we'll build it. If you'd prefer a different handling (e.g., skip entirely, or always include a thumbnail of the source page), let us know.
