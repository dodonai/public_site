Issue 1

? You decided

~300–500 words per operative report.

Issue 3

Verbatim extraction produced unreadable "grocery list" output

● fixed & live
! You reported
"This is a grocery list rather than a sentence… maintain PTO pain control fentanyl patch."

When we turned on verbatim extraction across the board, structured EHR content — medication administration records, checkbox forms, vitals tables — got dumped into the chronology as raw text fragments rather than readable narrative. Useful sections (HPI, Assessment) got the verbatim treatment they needed, but unreadable sections did too.

? You decided

Section-level hybrid voice. Verbatim only for HPI and Assessment within admission summaries, discharge summaries, and consultations. Summarize everything else, with these specific overrides from your email:

  • Primary Care HPI & Assessment → summarize
  • Urgent Care HPI & Assessment → summarize
  • Discharge Summary Plan/Disposition → summarize
  • Rehab (PT/OT/SLP) — every section → summarize

Tie-breaker rule: if a section header in the source is compound (e.g., "Assessment/Plan", "Impression/Plan"), the entire section is verbatim.

✓ We built

Updated all five affected templates: Primary Care, Urgent Care, Specialist Consult, Hospital Records, and Rehab. Each section now carries its own voice instruction (verbatim or narrative) according to the matrix you confirmed.

The Hospital template uses conditional voice based on the type of hospital note: an admission summary entry quotes HPI & Assessment verbatim, but a nursing note or progress note from the same admission summarizes everything.

The compound-header tie-breaker is encoded in the prompts so "Assessment/Plan" sections get treated as verbatim automatically.

3.Quick reference — voice matrix

A compact reference showing what gets quoted verbatim vs. summarized across all document types and sections. Cross-reference any chronology entry against this table to verify the voice is what you expect.

Document type Header / CC HPI / Subjective ROS & Exam Findings Assessment Plan / Disposition
Admission Summary within hospital records summarize VERBATIM summarize — VERBATIM summarize
Discharge Summary within hospital records summarize VERBATIM summarize — VERBATIM summarize
Consultation inpatient or outpatient summarize VERBATIM summarize — VERBATIM summarize
Primary Care PCP / family med summarize summarize summarize — summarize summarize
Urgent Care walk-in clinics summarize summarize summarize — summarize summarize
Progress Note daily inpatient notes summarize summarize summarize — summarize summarize
Nursing Note per-shift nursing summarize summarize summarize — — summarize
Imaging / MRI / CT / EMG diagnostic reports summarize — — summarize summarize —
Operative Report surgery narrative summarize — — summarize summarize summarize
PT / OT / Speech / Rehab summarize summarize summarize — summarize summarize
Labs single-line per lab 25-word summary per lab — test, value, ref range, abnormal/normal
Quoted verbatim from the record Summarized into narrative Not present in this doc type
Voice-rule summary

The underlying principle

Verbatim for plain-English clinical narrative authored by a human (the kind of writing that contains causation-anchoring statements). Summarize structured / checkbox / list-style content where verbatim extraction produces unreadable output. Compound section headers default to verbatim per your tie-breaker rule.

4.Sample chronology entry

A concrete example: this is what an admission summary entry will look like under the new rules. Verbatim portions are tagged verbatim; summarized portions are tagged summarized.

Sample entry — Admission Summary

10/29/2021 — Admission to University Hospital

Reason for Admission summarized

John Doe was admitted under the care of hospitalist Dr. Jennifer Lee for progressive bilateral lower-extremity weakness, paresthesias, and gait instability following recent influenza vaccination. (Ex. 14, p. 201.)

History of Present Illness verbatim

"63 y/o M presents with 1-week history of progressive bilateral foot numbness ascending into both legs, with worsening gait instability and lower extremity weakness. Pt reports onset approximately 7 days after influenza vaccination administered 10/15/2021. Pt denies recent GI illness, URI, trauma, or known toxic exposures. Endorses fatigue and difficulty climbing stairs. No prior episodes of similar neurologic symptoms." (Ex. 14, p. 206.)

Examination, Diagnostics, & Treatment summarized

Initial neurologic examination demonstrated bilateral lower-extremity weakness (4/5) with diminished patellar and Achilles reflexes. (Ex. 14, p. 209.) Lumbar puncture demonstrated elevated CSF protein with normal cell count consistent with albuminocytologic dissociation. (Ex. 14, p. 212.) Five-day course of IVIG was initiated. (Ex. 14, p. 215.)

Assessment verbatim

"Acute inflammatory demyelinating polyneuropathy, consistent with Guillain-Barré Syndrome. Onset within approximately one week of influenza vaccination, with no alternative inciting trigger identified. Findings of ascending weakness, hyporeflexia, abnormal EMG/NCS demonstrating demyelinating pattern, and elevated CSF protein support the diagnosis." (Ex. 14, p. 219.)

Plan & Disposition summarized

Treatment plan included completion of IVIG course, daily neurologic monitoring, physical and occupational therapy evaluations, fall precautions, and transition to inpatient rehabilitation. (Ex. 14, p. 221.)

The "gold" causation language from the assessment is preserved verbatim — quotable as-is for petition drafting. The repetitive boilerplate around exam findings, treatments, and admin details is summarized to keep the entry readable.
