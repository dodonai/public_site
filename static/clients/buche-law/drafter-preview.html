The full workflow

From the case file to a finished brief

The first two steps are the Dodonai workflow you run today. The brief is a new step that picks up right where your summary leaves off.

1
You

Upload the case file

Medical records, the denial report, anything in the file, into the matter.

Same as any Dodonai case · nothing changes
2
Dodonai

Generate the medical summary

The chronology you already rely on, built from the records.

Same as today · the brief reads from this
3
You

Click Generate brief

Right on the finished summary. Pick the claim type, then enter the case details: player, team, roster dates, and category.

4
Dodonai

Draft the brief

It reads the summary, ties every fact back to a record citation, scores the impairments against the 2025 Point Chart for LOD, and flags anything the record doesn't cover instead of guessing. Runs in the background and pings you when it's done.

5
You

Review and finalize

The Word doc lands in the matter next to the summary. Edit it like any draft, then file it.

A closer look at step 4

The LOD point values aren't written by the AI

Every impairment gets looked up in the 2025 Point Chart, and the total is added up by code. So the score and the 9-point threshold call are exact every time. Each distinct injury lands under its own category with the record citation behind it. If something doesn't map cleanly to the chart, it flags it for you instead of guessing.
