Workflow automation · 5 priorities identified in our meeting and emails

  1. Support inbox triage. The 10-person shared inbox blasts everyone, paralegals desensitize, and client emails get buried. An agent reads incoming mail, classifies it, and routes to the right one or two people.
  2. Cognito Forms to Filevine data bridge. Client questionnaire answers flow directly into the right Filevine fields, ready for merge. No retyping, cleaner data downstream.
  3. Affidavit generation from intake answers. Today: 1 to 2 hours per affidavit, paralegal converting Cognito answers into prose. Target: 15 minutes of attorney review on a generated draft.
  4. Demand letter and appeal letter generation. The four templates you sent Monday, generated from the claim file in one pass. Boilerplate handled, client-specific prose drafted, paralegal reviews highlighted sections.
  5. Deadline extraction from court orders, denial letters, faxes, and SSA decisions. Incoming documents read, party and dates extracted, deadlines surfaced to the Filevine calendar with a confirm step before writing.

How they connect

Both streams feed the same outcome: less time on data movement and prose assembly, more time on legal judgment. The document side cuts the 1 to 2 hours per affidavit and the editing pass on every demand letter. The workflow side feeds the document side with clean, structured data and surfaces deadlines before they slip.

Phase 1 · proposed scope 4 to 6 weeks

We'd recommend bundling these because they share the same data plumbing into Filevine.

Build A · Document Generation Engine

For the four templates you sent. Pulls structured data from Filevine, drafts the prose-heavy sections (medical chronology, severe impairments, occupation analysis, legal arguments), and produces a Word draft your paralegal reviews and finalizes.

What changes for your team:
  • Affidavit assembly: from 1 to 2 hours of transcription to about 15 minutes of review
  • Demand letters and appeals: boilerplate plus client-specific prose drafted in one pass
  • Damages calculations: the spreadsheet math runs automatically from policy and payment data

Build B · Support Inbox Triage Agent

Reads incoming support email, classifies it (new client, existing client, vendor, junk, urgent deadline, etc.), and routes to the right one or two people instead of blasting all ten.

What changes for your team:
  • No more "delete delete delete delete" across the whole firm
  • Client emails surface inside hours, not days
  • The paralegals get back attention they were spending on triage

Build C · Cognito Forms to Filevine Bridge

The questionnaire data your clients submit through Cognito flows directly into the right Filevine fields, ready for merge. No retyping.

What changes for your team:
  • About one person's day of data entry per week, returned
  • Cleaner data downstream (no transcription typos)
  • Affidavit and demand letter builds start with the data they need
