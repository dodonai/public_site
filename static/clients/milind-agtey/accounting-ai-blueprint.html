The firm keeps its report. The engine does the assembly.

The hard part

Getting the numbers right, every time

The product lives or dies on figure correctness, so correctness is built into the architecture, not bolted on after.

🔢Numbers computed in code, never by the AInon-negotiable
If the AI did the math
  • Plausible-looking but wrong figures.
  • No way to trace where a number came from.
  • In financial reporting, that is a liability.
→
How we build it
  • Every figure is computed by code, from the QuickBooks data.
  • The AI only writes about numbers the system already calculated.
  • Feature 1 is matched to a real hand-built report before anything ships on top.
The build question you asked

Excel plug-in, download, or hosted app?

Excel and Google Sheets stay as the output format. The question is where the engine that fills them runs. A hosted app is the only option that can hold a secure QuickBooks login and produce reports on a schedule.

Excel / Sheets add-in
lives in the spreadsheet

Great at: zero change to how people work

Where it breaks: cannot safely hold the QuickBooks login or run reports on a schedule

Local download
installed on one machine

Great at: data never leaves the desk

Where it breaks: no unattended monthly runs, hard to update, fragile login security

Hosted web app
Dodonai recommends

Great at: secure QuickBooks connection, scheduled reports, simple to update

Best fit when: you want a product you can sell, not a one-machine tool
