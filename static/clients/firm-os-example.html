What we heard

Three goals, in the firm's order

The firm named these on the discovery call. Everything in this brief is in service of these three — not the other way around.

Goal 01

Stay modern

Carriers will eventually expect AI and push rates down. The firm wants to be ahead of that line, not chasing it.

"We clearly want to stay with the times… make sure we're not falling behind."
Goal 02

Reduce the stress

A 90%-comp-defense practice at 100–120 emails a day is a treadmill. The point of automation is to get the partners off it.

"Reduce the chronic daily stress level… it's going to put me in the grave."
Goal 03

Increase profit

Recapture the admin hours the firm loses, and capture the compliant billable time the current process leaks.

"The third thing we want to do is we want to increase profits."
Admin lost, firm-wide~300 hrs/mo
By the firm's own estimate, every attorney loses about 20 non-billable hours a month to the same intake, filing, summarizing, and time-reconstruction. Across the firm that's ~300 hours a month — most of a full-time hire — spent on work that isn't law. The question isn't whether to automate it. It's who builds it, who owns it, and how fast the firm feels the difference.
The destination

One system, not a pile of subscriptions

A Firm OS: the firm runs on one coherent system it owns, instead of a stack of disconnected tools — consumer AI here, a legacy case manager there, the billing system over here — held together by each attorney clicking between them. The OS is the brain. It keeps the tools that earn their keep (the billing system, Dodonai) in one place, replaces the antiquated case manager, and runs the coordination that today eats the day — without stacking another subscription on top.
