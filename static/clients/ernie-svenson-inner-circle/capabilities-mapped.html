4. What doesn't map, and why that's fine

The whole legal-practice half of the catalog doesn't apply to you, because you're not running matters:

  • Legal drafting agents: appeals, complaints, demand and mediation letters, hearing prep, property division, estate-doc proofreading, discovery
  • Practice-management integrations: Clio, MyCase, Carrot
  • Legal intake-to-engagement, conflict checks, disbursement / QuickBooks / DocuSign close-loops
  • Litigation deadline parsing, court-filing prep, CLE and bar-rule monitoring

That half is exactly what your members buy from me directly, so it's no gap at all. Your demos don't need to cover the legal-drafting agents. Your referral does. You show them the Brand Brain and the content engine running in your business, they extrapolate to their firm, and the practice-specific build is the engagement I run with them. You sell the pattern, I build the specifics.

5. The sequence, so this doesn't become "everything at once"

You have a lot of platforms and a lot of ideas, which is exactly why this should be a sequenced menu, not a big bang:

  1. Brand Brain (foundation). Stand up the knowledge and voice layer. Small, fast, and everything else needs it. Doubles immediately as a demo.
  2. Content engine (Phase 2, already scoped). The master workflow, org-level for Cham. Your highest-impact build and your headline demo.
  3. Pick from the menu. Once 1 and 2 are running, choose what hurts most next: retention monitor, inbox triage, the dashboard, lead discovery. One at a time, each cancelable, each its own small build.

No long-term commitment. Each piece is a discrete decision: keep going, pause, or stop. The Brand Brain is yours to keep regardless.
