What we built for you

Visit-by-Visit Chronology

Chronology Summary

A date-ordered summary with one entry per visit or encounter. Each entry stays tight and readable — no diagnosis codes, no facility addresses, no appointment times, and only the treating clinician named.

What each entry includes:

  • DateThe date of the visit (date only — no times).
  • ProviderOnly physicians, nurse practitioners, and physician assistants are named — never techs, medical assistants, nurses, or whoever entered the note.
  • FacilityThe facility name only — addresses are left out.
  • Reason for visitThe presenting complaint or reason for the encounter.
  • SummaryA plain-language narrative of what happened — no ICD or billing codes.
  • Assessment / diagnosesDiagnoses discussed at the visit, in plain language.
  • Medications orderedAny medication, prescription, over-the-counter item, supplement, or vitamin ordered or changed at that visit, with the prescribing doctor.
  • PlanThe plan, orders, and follow-up.
Example entry
04/15/2026 — University Hospitals Internal Medicine — Thomas Wagner, DO
Reason: Follow-up, hypertension and fatigue.
Summary: Blood pressure improved on current regimen. Reports ongoing fatigue; ordered thyroid panel. Discussed diet and exercise.
Medications ordered: Lisinopril 10 mg daily — continued (Dr. Wagner); Vitamin D3 2000 IU daily — started (Dr. Wagner).
Plan: Recheck in 8 weeks; labs before next visit.

Standalone Summary Sections

High-level Overview

These four sections appear once at the top of the report and pull together information from across the whole record — so you don't have to re-read every visit to see the full picture. They are not repeated for each event.

Sections included:

  • MedicationsEvery medication, prescription, over-the-counter item, supplement, and vitamin in one list — with the prescribing doctor and any changes noted over time.
  • Illness HistoryThe history of illnesses documented in the record.
  • Past Medical HistoryPast conditions and diagnoses, each with its date of diagnosis where available.
  • Surgeries & ProceduresAll surgeries and procedures, each with its date.

Lab Results Timeline

Labs Over Time

Lab values are pulled out of the record and laid out as a timeline, so you can see how each result moves across dates at a glance — with the test name, the value, the normal range, and the ordering physician.

Example layout
Hemoglobin A1c — 7.4 (Jan) → 6.9 (Apr) → 6.5 (Jul)
LDL Cholesterol — 142 (Jan) → 118 (Jul)
Each row is a lab; each column is a date.

Diagnostic Studies

Imaging & Tests

Imaging and diagnostic tests (X-ray, MRI, CT, ultrasound, EKG, and the like) are grouped together and kept to the essentials — the date, the test name, the ordering physician, and the result.

Example entry
03/02/2026 — MRI Lumbar Spine — ordered by Thomas Wagner, DO
Result: Mild disc bulge at L4-L5 without nerve root compression.

Automatic Sorting & De-duplication

Behind the Scenes

Before any of the above is written, your uploaded records are split into individual documents, sorted by date, sorted into the right category (visit notes, labs, imaging), and checked for duplicates. Duplicate records and pages are dropped automatically, and administrative pages (face sheets, demographics, blank pages) are left out of the summary.

How to use it

  1. Open a matter and upload your medical records — a single combined PDF is fine, we'll split it into individual documents for you.
  2. Choose Poplar Mason Medical Records as the rule set, then click Generate.
  3. Open the finished report — the standalone sections (Medications, Illness History, Past Medical History, Surgeries & Procedures) come first, followed by the visit-by-visit chronology, the lab timeline, and any diagnostic studies, all formatted to your rules.
