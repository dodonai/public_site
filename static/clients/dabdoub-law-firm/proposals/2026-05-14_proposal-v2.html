How the System Improves Over Time

A fair question on May 7: can the platform learn and improve, or does it just reproduce existing work? Three loops compound.

Per-letter retrieval. Every appeal edited in Word becomes training data for the next one. The retrieval index re-indexes finalized letters on a cadence, so the closer A-16 gets to the firm's voice, the closer it stays. Edits aren't friction; they're the training signal.

Per-carrier patterns. The first carrier we cover end-to-end is the hardest. Each carrier we add after that (Cigna, Hartford, Unum, MetLife, Aetna) extends the previous carrier's pattern instead of starting from scratch. After two or three carriers the firm's argument library compounds.

The case-pattern library compounds. Long COVID has a different argument structure than ME/CFS, which has a different one than ADHD-cap-disclosure, which has a different one than lung-cancer-pre-existing. Every condition the firm wins teaches the system how to argue it.
