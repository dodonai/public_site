§1 · The frame

What this is, in one paragraph.

A working catalog of what Dodonai builds for law firms and professional-services firms. Not "here's what AI could theoretically do for your firm" — here are the agents and workflows already running in production at firms like yours, here's how we package them, here's how engagements actually unfold.

§2 · Executive summary

What Dodonai builds, architecturally.

AI agents that draft your firm's specific work, in your firm's specific voice, triggered by your firm's specific matter state — running on a foundation we help rationalize (kept tools, custom internal apps replacing narrow-use SaaS, bridges into the rest), with drafts landing back in your own folders for human review. The diagram below is the actual architecture we build at a firm; the catalog in §3 is the inventory that fills each layer.

The thesis: any repeatable workflow inside a professional-services firm — anything where a human reads context, applies judgment patterns from prior matters, and produces a draft — is a candidate for an AI agent that drafts the first 70–90% and hands off to the human for review and judgment.

Two ways to engage with the catalog in §3: packaged deliverables (§3a — fixed-scope bundles like the AI Discovery Audit, Firm Wiki, Modern AI Software Stack, Marketing Suite) or individual workflows (§3b–§3d — pick one agent at a time). Backed by the Dodonai SaaS platform (250+ active firm subscribers, productized medical-record / IME / chronology / depo work) and the internal agent stack — battle-tested on our own books before any client sees them, productized in §3c.

§6 · Compliance & data handling

The part most firms ask first.

The short version. A longer written compliance brief is available on request before signature.

Compliance posture
  • Retention. Dodonai does not retain firm data after processing. No persistent copies on Dodonai infrastructure beyond the operational lifecycle of an agent run.
  • No model training. Firm data is never used to train any model. Anthropic's API does not train on inputs by default.
  • Cloud infrastructure. Processing runs on Google Cloud Platform under Dodonai's account. Logging is scoped to operational metadata, not document content.
  • Tenant-resident option. For most engagements we run agents inside the firm's own Microsoft 365 / Google Workspace tenant — claim files, medical reviews, drafts route through Microsoft Graph / Google APIs to the LLM and return as .docx / .pdf in the firm's own SharePoint / Drive. No third-party storage.
  • HIPAA + SOC 2 posture. SOC 2-aligned controls; HIPAA-aware document handling; BAA available where required (e.g., medical-records summarization workflows).
  • Privilege. Every draft is human-reviewed before it leaves the firm. Privilege stays with the attorney; agents draft and prepare, the attorney reviews and files.
  • Vendor flexibility. When dictation is involved (intake recording → case summary), we support Whisper API direct (simpler stack, fewer parties to the data) or Fireflies with their BAA (richer feature set, additional contracted privacy layer). Either works.

  • Wave 4 — Litigation. Federal complaint drafting from denied-appeal claim file. JSR / PSO drafting. Mediation statement. Settlement demand letter with damages calc.
