Executive Summary

You're running a drone & aviation practice on a stack of disconnected SaaS — Lawmatics (~$453/mo) for intake and closing, Streak as a CRM, plus manual reconciliation of who signed, who paid, and under what name. The crop-dusting / drone-spraying exemption flow is ~80% of your business and today it runs through your hands: you review the intake, match the right contract and price, verify the name and address, type it into a Sheet, and hand it to staff. That's your ~$850/hr time spent on data entry.

We'll build you a Firm OS — one coherent system you own that converts leads to clients automatically, generates your deliverables from clean validated data, and alerts you the moment the FAA changes the paperwork. The outcome is positional as well as operational: Rupprecht Law becomes the most technologically capable drone-law practice in the country, with your two overseas staff redeployed from manual entry into managerial roles — one person handling 500–1,000 clients instead of 100.

The deeper outcome is leverage: lower input cost compounds on your rule-of-thirds, so you can price sharper or keep fatter margin, and the build pays for itself against the tools it retires and the hours it gives back.

Scope of Work

The engagement is structured as learn-alongside, not hand-off — you (and your technical staffer) end up understanding the system and able to iterate on it, with your codebase optionally connected to your own Claude so you can make tweaks and keep the keys.

Phase 0: Discovery & Architecture

Kicks off once this SOW is agreed. A focused architecture pass that turns the v1 scope above into a build spec: deep-dive working sessions on the spraying-exemption flow end-to-end, confirm the integration boundaries (Stripe, ConvertKit, Rsign, Gavel, Apps Script, Smith.AI, Twilio), and lock acceptance criteria for each pillar.

What you walk away with: a written Architecture Blueprint — each workflow mapped to a specific build step, integration requirements, and a build schedule — that you own regardless of whether we continue.

Satisfaction guarantee: if the Blueprint doesn't give you a clear, actionable roadmap, you're under no obligation to continue to the build.

The Math

The "~$3–5k/mo" you heard on 6/11 was hosting + a heavy support tier; "~$1k/mo if simple" was hosting + light support. Hosting alone is a few hundred a month — roughly what you're already cutting in Lawmatics and Streak — so your ongoing cost is near net-neutral from day one.

Assumptions & Out of Scope

We're assuming:

  • You'll provide access to the tools above within 5 business days of Phase 0 kickoff
  • Stripe, Rsign, ConvertKit, Smith.AI, and Twilio API capabilities are as documented — if a specific workflow is blocked by an API limitation, we'll surface alternatives during the Blueprint
  • During the build, you and your staff are available ~30 minutes/week for check-ins and testing feedback
  • Third-party pricing and APIs (Anthropic, Twilio, Stripe, your vendors) remain broadly stable — material changes outside our control may require re-scoping

Out of scope for v1 unless specifically added (out-of-scope items are new scope by mutual agreement, not absorbed):

  • Registration pipeline (Gavel) — Gavel stays untouched in v1; the OS hands off to it. Registration automation is Phase 2.
  • Defense-grade certification (FedRAMP, etc.) — the v1 sandbox is designed so this isn't precluded as defense work grows; formalizing it is a separate engagement when a client requires it.
  • Accounting / trust — N/A by design (100% upfront, non-refundable → no trust, no A/R).
  • Custom machine learning or model training
  • Auto-sending of contracts, filings, or payments on your behalf — agents draft and prepare; you review and approve
  • Any workflow requiring regulated professional judgment

Security & Compliance

The platform baseline is HIPAA-compliant and SOC 2-certified, with all storage on Google Cloud (US data residency) and encryption at rest and in transit. Your export-controlled / ITAR matters get a lockable sandbox (Phase 1d) with gated access you control — built "good now, perfect later" so it ships without stalling the rest of the build.
