Executive summary

The engine, on a system you own

When Tatiana leaves on July 31, the administrative engine of the practice goes with her — scheduling, chasing records, getting them into Dodonai and back to you, and the invoicing follow-up that insurers do everything to stall. Today that engine also runs across more tools than it needs to: Practice Panther as a record store and biller, QuickBooks for invoicing, manual calendaring, and roughly two hours of your own time per matter in Claude.

We propose to build you a Firm OS — a single platform you own that runs the administrative front-end automatically, files into the OneDrive structure you already use, and surfaces only the handful of decisions that genuinely need a physician. Phase 1 is live and tested before Tatiana's last day. The clinical report pipeline you run in Claude today comes second, on your timeline — committed now, started when you're ready.

The deeper outcome is positional: Greenspoon Consulting becomes a practice that runs on a system instead of on one person's knowledge. The next person inherits a platform, not a folder of instructions — and as your son and colleagues take on more IME and CME work, the same OS extends to them.

Weeks until handoff~6
The open question isn't whether to automate — Tatiana already documented the workflow. It's who builds it, who owns it, and whether the practice keeps running after July 31 without you re-hiring an operations person or spending your nights wiring scenarios together yourself.
