What is eDiscovery Software?

eDiscovery, short for electronic discovery, refers to the process in which legal professionals identify, collect, and produce electronically stored information (ESI) as evidence in legal cases. This includes emails, documents, presentations, databases, voicemail, audio and video files, social media, and websites. The eDiscovery process is typically used in litigation, investigations, and Freedom of Information Act requests. It involves several stages, including identification, preservation, collection, processing, review, and production of digital data. eDiscovery has become increasingly important as the volume of electronic data grows and plays a crucial role in legal proceedings today.
